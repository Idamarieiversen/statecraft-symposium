Symposium · April 2026

Statecraft, Sovereignty
& Digital Government

From early e-government initiatives in the 1990s to today's experiments with AI, states have long sought to harness digital technologies to reimagine how they interact with citizens. This two-day symposium explores digital sovereignty, emerging forms of digital statecraft, and how these relate to existing digital government and digital transformation initiatives.

§ 03

Practical Information

Venue

Goldsmiths, University of London, New Cross, London SE14 6NW. Specific room details will be confirmed closer to the event.

Getting There

Goldsmiths is best reached by Overground to New Cross Gate, or by bus. It is approximately 15 minutes from London Bridge by train. Full travel instructions will be circulated to all participants in advance.

Accommodation

We recommend booking accommodation early. A list of suggested hotels will be circulated. Accommodation costs are not covered by the organisers unless you have been awarded the travel bursary.

Catering

Lunch is provided on both days via voucher. Coffee and drinks are self-paid — a café will be open on site. If you have dietary requirements that were not noted in your RSVP, please contact the organisers as soon as possible.

Format

The symposium is fully in-person. Remote participation or presentation is not possible. All accepted participants are expected to present in person.

Visa & Invitation Letters

If you are travelling from outside the UK, please remember to check visa requirements well in advance. If you require a formal invitation letter, please contact Ida at idiv@itu.dk and allow sufficient processing time ahead of your visa appointment.
