Statecraft, Sovereignty
& Digital Government

From early e-government initiatives in the 1990s to today's experiments with AI, states have long sought to harness digital technologies to reimagine how they interact with citizens. This two-day symposium explores digital sovereignty, emerging forms of digital statecraft, and how these relate to existing digital government and digital transformation initiatives.

§ 01

About the Symposium

The 2010s saw many countries begin to collaborate on what is often referred to as "digital transformation," drawing ideological inspiration from broader shifts associated with Web 2.0. Terms such as collaboration, participation, and openness gained traction and helped shape a new vision of digital government, as well as an international community committed to its realisation.

Beyond international collaboration and benchmarking, Estonia has used its "Digital Estonia" initiative as a form of post-Soviet nation branding, while in Ukraine, "digitalisation" has been reoriented around needs emerging from war. These examples illustrate how national digital government strategies are both shaped by and play out on an international stage — and can be fruitfully understood as forms of digital statecraft.

Digital government initiatives also frequently involve commercial partnerships. Such dependencies now unfold within a highly turbulent geopolitical context marked by democratic backsliding and growing concern about digital sovereignty. How should we think about digital government and the provision of public services in light of these changing geopolitical and technological contexts?

We are planning an edited volume or special issue, and hope the symposium will serve as the foundation for a lasting network of researchers and practitioners.

We welcome presentations from fields including — but not limited to — Science and Technology Studies, software and platform studies, media studies, international relations, political theory, and cultural studies.

§ 04

Practical Information

Venue

Goldsmiths, University of London, New Cross, London SE14 6NW. Specific room details will be confirmed closer to the event.

Getting There

Goldsmiths is best reached by Overground to New Cross Gate, or by bus. It is approximately 15 minutes from London Bridge by train. Full travel instructions will be circulated to all participants in advance.

Accommodation

We recommend booking accommodation early. A list of suggested hotels will be circulated. Accommodation costs are not covered by the organisers unless you have been awarded the travel bursary.

Catering

Lunch and refreshments are provided on both days. If you have dietary requirements that were not noted in your RSVP, please contact the organisers as soon as possible.

Format

The symposium is fully in-person. Remote participation or presentation is not possible. All accepted participants are expected to present in person.

Visa & Invitation Letters

If you require an official invitation letter for visa purposes, please contact the organisers directly and allow sufficient processing time ahead of your visa appointment.
